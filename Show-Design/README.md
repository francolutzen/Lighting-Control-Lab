# Show Design

Show Design explores the creative principles used to transform lighting systems into visual experiences.

While Foundations explains how lighting technology works and Lighting-Control explains how to operate it, this section focuses on why specific visual decisions are made.

The goal is not simply to control fixtures, but to design intentional visual experiences that support emotions, music, atmosphere and audience engagement.

## Purpose

The purpose of this section is to study the design principles behind lighting.

Topics may include:

* Color theory
* Psychology of color
* Visual rhythm
* Contrast
* Balance
* Tension and release
* Energy management
* Musical structure
* Audience perception
* Spatial awareness
* Atmosphere creation

These concepts help transform technical lighting control into meaningful visual design.

## Design Philosophy

Lighting is a communication tool.

Color, movement, brightness and timing influence how people perceive a space and how they emotionally respond to an environment.

A lighting designer is not only deciding which fixtures to activate, but also:

* What emotions to reinforce.
* What visual focus to create.
* How energy should rise and fall.
* How the audience should experience a moment.

Understanding these principles allows lighting to become part of the overall experience rather than simply decoration.

## Relationship to Other Sections
