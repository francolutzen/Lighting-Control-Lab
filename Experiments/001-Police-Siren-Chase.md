# Experiment 001 — Police Siren Chase

## Objective

Create a police vehicle lighting effect using:

- 2 LM30A Mini Spider 8×3W RGBW
- 2 Generic FG-369 RGB PAR
- DMX192 Controller

The objective is to explore how color, movement and visual layering can create strong real-world associations.

Rather than reproducing only the red and blue emergency lights, this experiment attempts to recreate the visual identity of a police vehicle by combining emergency lighting with simulated vehicle headlights.

---

## Inspiration

The experiment is inspired by police emergency vehicles and the visual language commonly associated with authority, urgency and law enforcement.

The effect is specifically intended for musical moments where lyrics make direct or indirect references to:

- Police
- Law enforcement
- Authority
- Emergency situations
- Pursuit or escape
- Criminal activity

## Design Concept

The effect is built using multiple visual layers.

### Layer 1 — Vehicle Headlights

The two FG-369 PAR fixtures provide a strong white wash intended to represent the front headlights of a police vehicle.

This layer establishes the visual presence of the vehicle itself and increases realism.

### Layer 2 — Emergency Lights

The two LM30A Mini Spider fixtures alternate between red and blue.

These colors reproduce the visual language most commonly associated with police emergency lighting.

### Layer 3 — Motion

The movement generated by the Mini Spider fixtures adds energy and realism.

Crossing beams, directional changes and alternating colors help simulate the dynamic behavior of a moving emergency vehicle.

Together, these layers create a more recognizable and immersive effect than using red and blue colors alone.

## Hypothesis

Adding simulated headlights to the traditional red-blue siren effect will increase realism and strengthen audience recognition of the police vehicle concept.

The experiment explores whether layered visual design produces stronger symbolic associations than color alone.

---

## Observations

Even with only four fixtures, the combination of white, red and blue immediately suggests the presence of a police vehicle.

The white PAR fixtures help establish the vehicle's physical presence, while the moving red and blue beams attract attention and communicate urgency.

The Mini Spider fixtures significantly increase realism by introducing movement and directional energy that static fixtures cannot easily reproduce.
