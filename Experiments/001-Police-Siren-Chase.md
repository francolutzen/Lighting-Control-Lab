## Objective

Create a lighting effect inspired by the visual identity of police emergency vehicles to explore how color, movement and visual layering can generate immediate real-world associations.

Rather than reproducing only the traditional red and blue warning lights, this experiment investigates how multiple visual layers can recreate the perceived presence of an emergency vehicle and reinforce lyrical or narrative moments related to authority, pursuit, crime or emergency situations.

---

This experiment is inspired by police emergency vehicles and the visual language commonly associated with authority, urgency and law enforcement.

The atmosphere is intended for musical moments where lyrics make direct or indirect references to:

## Design Concept

The atmosphere is constructed through several independent visual layers that together recreate the visual identity of an active police vehicle.

Rather than simply alternating red and blue colors, the experiment attempts to reproduce how a police vehicle is visually perceived in real life.

---

### Layer 1 — Vehicle Presence

The first layer represents the physical presence of the vehicle.

A strong white light simulates vehicle headlights, creating the impression that an object is occupying physical space rather than only projecting colored light.

This layer establishes realism before emergency lighting is introduced.

---

### Layer 2 — Emergency Identity

The second layer reproduces the visual language universally associated with police emergency lighting.

Primary palette:

* Red
* Blue

Rapid alternation between both colors immediately communicates authority, urgency and emergency without requiring additional contextual information.

Because these colors possess strong cultural recognition, audience identification occurs almost instantaneously.

---

### Layer 3 — Dynamic Motion

Movement transforms static warning lights into the perception of an active vehicle.

Rather than remaining fixed, beams sweep, cross and change direction to simulate:

* vehicle movement
* environmental scanning
* emergency response
* changing spatial focus

Motion increases realism while maintaining continuous visual energy.

---

### Layer 4 — Atmospheric Volume

Atmospheric haze or fog enhances the illusion of physical space.

Instead of merely increasing beam visibility, suspended particles allow the emergency colors and moving beams to occupy volume, producing a more immersive representation of a police vehicle moving through darkness.

---

## Design Considerations

### Symbol Recognition

Red and blue constitute one of the most recognizable emergency color combinations worldwide.

Even when presented abstractly through lighting, these colors immediately evoke concepts such as:

* authority
* urgency
* law enforcement
* danger
* intervention

The experiment explores how quickly audiences establish these associations.

---

### Visual Layering

Instead of relying exclusively on color, realism emerges through the combination of independent visual functions.

Vehicle presence establishes physical identity.

Emergency colors communicate symbolic meaning.

Movement suggests active behavior.

Atmospheric haze reinforces spatial immersion.

Separating these layers produces a richer visual composition than using red and blue alternation alone.

---

### Behavioral Evolution

Unlike traditional programmed chases that repeat indefinitely, this experiment treats the police vehicle as a dynamic entity capable of changing behavior.

Different visual states represent different operational situations while preserving the same visual identity.

## Hypothesis

Combining vehicle presence, emergency colors, movement and atmospheric volume into independent visual layers will generate stronger recognition of a police vehicle than using red and blue alternation alone.

The experiment also explores whether audiences instinctively associate these visual cues with authority, urgency and law enforcement even without explicit narrative context.
